Introduction:

Hey, this is a study project on scrollytelling, which involves a combination of scrolling and motion. In other words, it's the storytelling through the scrolling of our device screens.

Acknowledgments:

I'd like to express my sincere thanks to Pavol Bielik, CTO of LatticeFlow, for generously taking the time to guide me in studying d3.js, which has enhanced my skills in web animations along with THREE.js, P5.js, and GSAP.js.

Concept:

The idea behind the study involves animating SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) by manipulating the DOM through JavaScript using d3.js, along with the scroll event.

So we can manipulate our SVG, changing colors, positions, and shapes, adding movement to the content, thus creating a form of storytelling

Web interaction:

Web interactions are crucial, not just for grabbing attention but for crafting an immersive environment. To do so, we need to grasp how to manipulate the DOM, what SVG and canvas are, and there are also libraries like GSAP for animations, THREE.js for 3D on canvas, and p5.js for graphic manipulation with multiple re-renders to assist us.
